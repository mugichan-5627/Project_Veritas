FINANCIAL SHENANIGANS — FORENSIC ACCOUNTING FRAMEWORK
Source: Schilit, H. & Perler, J. "Financial Shenanigans" 
(McGraw-Hill, 4th Edition)
Purpose: Quality of Earnings analysis for PE due diligence

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #1 — RECORDING REVENUE TOO SOON
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Revenue recorded before it is earned or 
before the earnings process is complete.

Red Flags to check in target company filings:
- Revenue growing faster than cash collections
  (Accounts Receivable growing faster than Revenue)
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) increasing year on year
- Large revenue spikes in final quarter of fiscal year
- Bill-and-hold arrangements disclosed in footnotes
- Channel stuffing: inventory builds at distributors

PE Diligence Tests:
  DSO = (Accounts Receivable / Revenue) x 365
  Flag if DSO increases >10 days year on year
  
  Revenue Quality Ratio = Cash from Operations / Net Income
  Flag if ratio < 0.8 consistently

Formula: AR Growth Rate vs Revenue Growth Rate
  If AR Growth > Revenue Growth by >15%: RED FLAG

Source: Schilit Chapter 2, pp.25-67

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #2 — RECORDING BOGUS REVENUE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Revenue recorded that does not represent 
legitimate arm's length transactions.

Red Flags:
- Related party transactions not at market rates
- Round-trip transactions (cash sent out, cash received back)
- Barter transactions recorded at inflated values
- Revenue from sale of assets booked as operating revenue

PE Diligence Tests:
- Map all related party transactions from footnotes
- Compare related party revenue % to total revenue trend
- Check if major customers are also investors/promoters

Source: Schilit Chapter 3, pp.68-95

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #3 — BOOSTING INCOME WITH ONE-TIME ITEMS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Non-recurring gains presented as if they 
are part of normal operating performance.

Red Flags:
- Gains on asset sales included in operating income
- Litigation settlement gains boosting reported profit
- Pension income inflating operating earnings
- Restructuring charge reversals boosting income

PE Adjustment Required (EBITDA Normalisation):
  Adjusted EBITDA = Reported EBITDA
    - One-time gains
    + One-time charges (add back)
    + Stock-based compensation
    + Non-recurring restructuring costs
    - Capitalised expenses that should be opex

This is the "Quality of Earnings" adjustment that PE 
firms require before accepting reported EBITDA figures.

Source: Schilit Chapter 4, pp.96-128

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #4 — SHIFTING CURRENT EXPENSES TO LATER
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Costs that should be expensed immediately 
are capitalised as assets, boosting current profits.

Red Flags:
- Capitalised software development costs growing rapidly
- Capitalised customer acquisition costs
- R&D capitalisation without clear asset definition
- Depreciation life extended without business reason

PE Diligence Tests:
  Capitalisation Rate = Capex / (Capex + Operating Expenses)
  Flag if rising year on year without revenue justification
  
  Compare depreciation life to industry peers:
  If company uses 15yr life vs peer average of 8yr: RED FLAG

Source: Schilit Chapter 5, pp.129-158

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #5 — FAILING TO RECORD LIABILITIES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Obligations are kept off the balance sheet 
or are underreported.

Red Flags:
- Operating leases not capitalised (pre-Ind AS 116)
- Contingent liabilities buried in footnotes
- Pension obligations underfunded
- Warranty reserves declining while sales grow
- Deferred revenue declining unexpectedly

PE Adjustment Required:
  Adjusted Net Debt = Reported Net Debt
    + Capitalised Operating Leases (8x annual rent)
    + Unfunded pension obligations
    + Contingent liabilities (probability weighted)

Source: Schilit Chapter 6, pp.159-189

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #6 — SHIFTING CURRENT REVENUE TO LATER
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Revenue is deferred artificially to smooth 
earnings or manage analyst expectations.

Red Flags:
- Deferred revenue growing faster than billings
- Revenue recognition policy changes in footnotes
- Unusual growth in contract liabilities

PE Context: Less common in Indian mid-market but 
relevant for SaaS, subscription, and project-based 
businesses.

Source: Schilit Chapter 7, pp.190-210

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SHENANIGAN #7 — SHIFTING FUTURE EXPENSES TO CURRENT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Definition: Taking large charges now to clear the path 
for artificially clean future earnings.

Red Flags:
- Large restructuring charges every 2-3 years
- "Kitchen sink" write-offs in transition quarters
- Acquisition accounting used to create cookie jar reserves
- In-process R&D write-offs at acquisition

PE Context: Watch for this in carve-outs and turnarounds 
where management has incentive to reset the baseline.

Source: Schilit Chapter 8, pp.211-235

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CASH FLOW SHENANIGANS — THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Companies manipulate operating cash flow (OCF) because 
sophisticated investors focus on OCF over net income.

Key Manipulation Techniques:
1. Boosting OCF by slowing payables (extending supplier 
   payment terms inflates short-term OCF)
2. Securitising receivables (moves AR off balance sheet,
   boosting OCF artificially)
3. Classifying operating cash outflows as investing 
   (capex misclassification)

PE Forensic Test — Cash Conversion Quality:
  FCF Conversion = Free Cash Flow / EBITDA
  Healthy range: 40-60% for Indian mid-market
  Flag if: FCF Conversion < 30% for 2+ consecutive years
  
  Working Capital Intensity = 
    (AR + Inventory - AP) / Revenue
  Flag if increasing year on year (cash being consumed 
  by operations, not generated)

Source: Schilit Chapters 9-11, pp.236-310

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PE DUE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST — FINANCIAL SHENANIGANS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Run these 8 tests on every target company:

Test 1: Revenue Quality
  Cash from Ops / Net Income > 0.8? 
  YES = clean | NO = investigate

Test 2: Receivables Health  
  AR Growth vs Revenue Growth within 15%?
  YES = clean | NO = possible revenue manipulation

Test 3: EBITDA Normalisation
  Adjusted EBITDA calculated removing all one-time items?
  Document every adjustment with source

Test 4: Off-Balance Sheet Liabilities
  Operating leases capitalised at 8x annual rent?
  Contingent liabilities probability-weighted?

Test 5: Capitalisation Policy
  Capex ratio benchmarked against sector peers?
  Depreciation life vs industry standard?

Test 6: Related Party Transactions
  All RPTs mapped and assessed for arm's-length pricing?
  RPT revenue % trend — increasing or stable?

Test 7: Cash Conversion
  FCF / EBITDA > 30% for last 3 years?
  Working capital intensity stable or improving?

Test 8: Accounting Policy Changes
  Any revenue recognition or depreciation policy 
  changes in last 3 years? If yes — why?

INDIAN CONTEXT — ADDITIONAL RED FLAGS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Specific to Indian listed and unlisted companies:

1. Promoter pledge: High promoter share pledge 
   indicates financial stress — check BSE disclosures
   Flag if promoter pledge > 30% of holding

2. Related party complexity: Indian family-owned 
   businesses often have complex RPT structures
   across group entities — map the full group

3. Auditor changes: Auditor resignation or change 
   to lesser-known firm is a major red flag in India

4. MCA filing gaps: Check if annual returns filed 
   on time with Ministry of Corporate Affairs
   Late filings indicate governance issues

5. Contingent liabilities from tax disputes: 
   Indian companies frequently have large income 
   tax and GST disputes — assess probability of loss

These are not in Schilit (US-focused) but are 
critical for Indian PE diligence.
Applied by Indian PE funds: Kedaara, ChrysCapital, 
Warburg Pincus India, Blackstone India.
